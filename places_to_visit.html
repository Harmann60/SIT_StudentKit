Places to visit


Shaniwar Wada

Built in 1732, Shaniwar Wada was the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire. Though much of the fort was destroyed in a fire, its massive stone walls, majestic gateways, and lush gardens still attract visitors. The sound-and-light show here brings alive the Maratha history

Aga Khan Palace

Constructed in 1892 by Sultan Aga Khan III, this palace is an important landmark in Pune’s history. It served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and his wife Kasturba during the freedom struggle. Today, it houses a museum with photos, artifacts, and memorials of Gandhi’s life.

Sinhagad Fort

Situated about 30 km from Pune, Sinhagad Fort has witnessed many battles, the most famous being the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670. Its rugged climb and panoramic views make it a favorite among trekkers and history enthusiasts alike.

Pataleshwar Cave Temple

An 8th-century rock-cut temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the heart of Pune. The temple is carved out of a single basalt rock and reflects the architectural style of the Rashtrakuta dynasty

Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum

This unique museum showcases the collection of Dr. D.G. Kelkar, including over 20,000 artifacts—ranging from traditional lamps and musical instruments to exquisite sculptures, ornaments, and household items that reflect India’s cultural heritage.

Sarasbaug Ganpati Temple

Located in the serene Sarasbaug garden, this Ganesh temple is one of the most visited religious places in Pune. The temple has a history of over 200 years and remains a spiritual and cultural hub for devotees.

Parvati Hill

At 2100 feet above sea level, Parvati Hill offers a panoramic view of Pune city. The hill houses temples dedicated to Parvati, Vishnu, and Devdeveshwar, and is a popular spot for both devotees and tourists.

Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple

One of the most famous Ganesh temples in India, visited by lakhs of devotees every year. The temple’s grandeur during the Ganesh festival is unmatched, with elaborate decorations and rituals.

Khadakwasla Dam

Just 20 km from Pune, this picturesque dam is a popular weekend getaway. It serves as a picnic spot with serene waters, cool breeze, and roadside eateries offering local snacks like bhutta (roasted corn) and misal pav.

Osho Ashram (Osho International Meditation Resort)

Located in Koregaon Park, the Osho Ashram is a world-renowned meditation center offering wellness programs, therapy sessions, and peaceful gardens. It attracts visitors from across the globe seeking inner peace.

National War Memorial Southern Command

ndia’s only war memorial built by citizens, it commemorates the soldiers who sacrificed their lives post-independence. The sound and light show narrating India’s military history is a key attraction.

Pune Okayama Friendship Garden (Pu La Deshpande Garden)

nspired by the 300-year-old Korakuen Garden in Okayama, Japan, this beautifully landscaped garden symbolizes Indo-Japanese friendship. It features flowing streams, bridges, and lush greenery.

Mulshi Dam

Nestled amidst the Sahyadri hills, Mulshi Dam is a scenic spot popular for picnics, photography, and short treks. During monsoons, the area comes alive with waterfalls and misty valleys.

Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park

Spread across 130 acres, this zoo houses a wide variety of animals including tigers, leopards, elephants, and reptiles. The Katraj Snake Park within is a major attraction, known for its rich collection of snakes, including a King Cobra.

Bund Garden

Built by Sir Jamshedji Jeejeebhoy, this well-maintained garden is ideal for evening walks and picnics. The boating facility on the Mula-Mutha River adds to its charm.

Lavasa

A planned hill city near Pune, Lavasa is known for its colorful European-style buildings, lakeside promenade, adventure activities, and scenic views, making it a perfect weekend destination.

Pashan Lake

A man-made lake surrounded by greenery, Pashan Lake is a serene spot for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Migratory birds visit the lake during winter, making it a photographer’s delight.

Shivneri Fort

About 90 km from Pune, Shivneri Fort is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort has strong fortifications, water tanks, and the temple of Goddess Shivai Devi, after whom Shivaji was named.

Empress Garden

Spread over 39 acres, this botanical garden is home to rare trees, flower plants, and a variety of birds. It also hosts flower shows and cultural events.
